Technical Writing Skills Assignment

  1. What is SSL?

    SSL (Secure Sockets Layer) is a technology that keeps your internet connection secure by encrypting data sent between your browser and a website. This prevents hackers from stealing sensitive information like passwords or credit card numbers.

  2. What is TLS?

    TLS (Transport Layer Security) is the newer, more secure version of SSL. While we still say "SSL" in casual terms, modern websites actually use TLS to protect your data.

  3. How can I tell that my webpage is secure?

    Look for a padlock icon in the address bar of your browser and make sure the website's URL starts with https://. These are signs that the website is using SSL/TLS encryption.

  4. What does it mean to be secure?

    Being secure means that the data you send to and receive from a website is encrypted, making it hard for hackers to intercept or read it.

  5. What is encryption and why do I care?

    Encryption scrambles your data so only the intended recipient can understand it. You care because it protects your sensitive information (like passwords or payment details) from being stolen.

  7. Do the prefixes HTTP or HTTPS matter when we're talking about secure websites?

    Yes! HTTP is not secure, meaning your data can be intercepted. HTTPS is secure and uses SSL/TLS encryption to protect your information.

  8. What is an SSL certificate authority?

    An SSL certificate authority (CA) is a trusted organization that issues SSL certificates to websites. These certificates prove that a website is legitimate and secure.
